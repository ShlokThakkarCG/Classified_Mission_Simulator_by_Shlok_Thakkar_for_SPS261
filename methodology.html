Project Methodology

An overview of the simulation's design and academic framework.

Simulation Framework

The Classified Mission Simulator (CMS) is a choice-based narrative simulation designed to explore the complex trade-offs inherent in managing national security space programs. It models the delicate balance between government secrecy, public accountability, fiscal responsibility, and international relations in the context of space militarization.

Core Variables

The simulation operates on four primary metrics, each representing a key stakeholder or condition:

  • Public Trust: Represents the public's confidence in the government's transparency and competence. High secrecy or perceived failures will lower this metric, while successful, transparent actions can raise it.
  • System Resilience: A measure of the security and robustness of space assets. This includes defenses against cyber-attacks, physical threats, and espionage.
  • Diplomatic Stability: Reflects the state of international relations. Aggressive postures or unilateral secret actions can destabilize this metric, while cooperation and diplomacy can improve it.
  • Budget: A finite resource representing the program's funding. Every major decision has a cost, forcing the player to make fiscally-constrained choices.

Decision Modeling

Each "mission" presents the player (acting as the Director) with a scenario and a set of discrete choices. These choices are drawn from real-world policy debates surrounding:

  • Dual-use technology disclosure.
  • Rules of engagement for orbital assets.
  • Responses to unidentified objects or rival nation activities.
  • Public relations strategies following a visible incident.

The consequences of each choice are propagated through the game state, altering the four core metrics. The simulation's narrative branches based on these outcomes, leading to one of three final states: Balanced Success, Instability Warning, or Complete Disaster.
